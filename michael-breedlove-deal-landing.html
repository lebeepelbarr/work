The story

A vacant house. A missing heir. A closing that changed a life.

You already know the numbers, but the numbers are the smallest part of this one. Here’s how it actually happened.

  1. The drive-by

    The house had clearly been empty for years. Since Katrina, it turned out: two decades of vacancy, storm damage that was never repaired, and an owner nobody had seen. A vacancy that old almost always means a problem nobody has solved, so Michael went looking for the problem.

  2. Finding the heir

    The owner had passed away. Her only daughter was estranged and didn’t even know her mother was gone. With no seller to call, Michael worked the block instead: neighbors, family friends, anyone who remembered the family, until the trail finally led to her.

  3. The succession

    The daughter had inherited a house she never knew was hers. Michael guided her through the succession process, kept the closing smooth, and paid her over $100,000. The contract then went to a buyer ready to take on the $150,000 renovation.

  4. Closed, then the extra mile

    Twenty-one days after the lead, the deal closed with a $41,000 assignment fee. Then Michael kept going: he helped her open a bank account so she could cash the check, introduced her to a friend who sold her a car, and connected her with a CPA to catch up on her taxes.

Michael’s golden rule “Don’t be afraid to go the extra mile when helping solve problems. Vacant properties, especially ones that have been vacant for two decades, usually mean there are big problems.” Michael Breedlove · Real estate investor · Metairie, LA
