How Evan saved the deal

• Why he wholesaled it: fire-damage rehabs mean hairy paperwork and months before profit. Evan ran the opportunity cost and took the faster, cleaner assignment.
• The two saves: the mortgage company miscalculated the payoff, then the buyer’s lender refused to fund the fee. Evan helped coordinate a cash-out refi on the buyer’s other rentals so he could pay cash.
• The conversation most wholesalers avoid: rather than hide the number, Evan walked the buyer through the full $210,000 fee. The numbers worked, so the fee survived.
