The strategy behind the deal

• Why speed came first: the seller lived out of the area and was in town for the day. A 60-second response and an appointment within the hour got the property under contract before another investor had a chance.
• Why he wholesaled it: a $400,000 fire rehab means insurance paperwork and months before profit. A clean assignment got him paid and put the capital straight back to work.
• The math: ARV was around $1,400,000 with about $400,000 of work. A $500,000 buy left enough room for an end buyer to take it down and still make money. That room paid the $210,000 fee, and the buyer's full math is on the breakdown page.

What you can learn from this

“Be transparent with your buyers. If the numbers work for them, then they shouldn’t have a problem with your fee.”

— Evan Rocha
• The deal nearly died twice. First a payoff miscalculation, then a lender who refused to fund the fee. How each one got solved is in the breakdown.
• Honesty is a closing tool. Evan showed the buyer the full $210,000 instead of hiding the spread. The numbers still worked, so the deal, and the relationship, survived.
