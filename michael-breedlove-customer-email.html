How Michael solved the impossible

• Why he wholesaled it: the house needed a $150,000 renovation. Assigning the contract to a buyer built for that work meant a faster, cleaner payday and no construction risk.
• Finding the missing heir: the owner had passed away, and her estranged daughter didn’t know. With no seller to call, Michael worked the neighborhood: neighbors, family friends, anyone who remembered the family.
• Unlocking ownership: he guided the heir through the succession process, kept the closing smooth, and she walked away with over $100,000 for a house she never knew was hers.

Lessons worth stealing

“Don’t be afraid to go the extra mile when helping solve problems. Vacant properties, especially ones that have been vacant for two decades, usually mean there are big problems.”

— Michael
• Old vacancies are unsolved problems. Twenty years empty meant title, succession, and family knots nobody had untangled. The investor who solves the problem earns the deal.
• The deal ends at closing. The reputation doesn’t. Michael helped her open a bank account, buy a car, and catch up on her taxes with a CPA. None of it was required. All of it matters.
