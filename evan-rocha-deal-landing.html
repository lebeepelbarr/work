The story

Sixty seconds to answer. Twenty-one days to $210,000.

You already know the fee. Here’s how the deal nearly died twice, and what Evan did instead of panicking.

  1. Speed to lead

    The seller lived out of the area and was in town for a single day. The lead came in, Evan’s team made contact within 60 seconds, and he was sitting at the appointment within the hour. There was no callback window and no second chance: whoever answered slower wasn’t getting this deal.

  2. The payoff surprise

    The property was complicated from the start: a fire-damaged multifamily spread across two parcels, with the seller’s insurance company in the mix. Then the mortgage company miscalculated the payoff, and the whole deal nearly came apart in escrow.

  3. The fee problem

    The buyer’s lender refused to fund the $210,000 assignment fee. Instead of losing the buyer, Evan helped coordinate a cash-out refinance on the buyer’s other rentals so he could pay cash. And rather than hide the number, he sat the buyer down and walked him through the full fee.

  4. Closed

    The numbers still worked for the buyer, so the fee survived the conversation. $210,000 in 21 days: ten thousand dollars a day, on a deal most investors would have watched fall apart.

Evan’s golden rule “Be transparent with your buyers. If the numbers work for them, then they shouldn’t have a problem with your fee.” Evan Rocha · Real estate investor · Fresno, CA
